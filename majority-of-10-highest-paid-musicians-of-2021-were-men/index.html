Majority Of 10 Highest-Paid Musicians Of 2021 Were Men

If you’re a music lover, you’ll be interested to know who the highest-paid musicians of 2021 are. According to recent reports, Bruce Springsteen tops 2021’s highest-paid musicians list, Jay-Z comes in second — without selling catalog and Taylor Swift being the only woman in 10 Highest-Paid Musicians of 2021.

Keep reading for the full list!

10 Taylor Swift ($80 million)

The only women on the list. Swift’s vengeful actions helped her land in the top 10 despite her catalog being famously sold out from under her. She’s been rerecording and rereleasing her earlier albums, with two of them being re-released this year. Both albums reached the top of the charts, with Red (Taylor’s Version) and Fearless (Taylor’s Version) respectively reaching the No. 2 and No. 4 albums in terms of total sales in 2021. Partnerships with Peloton and Starbucks boosted her bank account even further.

9 Blake Shelton ($83 million)

Shelton, the only country singer on the list, received a windfall of more than $50 million for a previously undisclosed catalog sale. Even in the face of a hard economic landscape, he boosted his profits with an eight-figure salary for The Voice, a new album called Body Language, and a tour that made $14.5 million.

8 Mötley Crüe ($95 million)

According to insiders, Mötley Crüe’s cut was closer to $90 million than the $150 million widely reported when news of the band’s master-recordings sale to BMG surfaced. It’s still a long journey, made possible by the heavy metal legends’ uncommon ability to keep their masters till now.

7 Lindsey Buckingham ($100 million)

Buckingham signed a similar-sized agreement with Hipgnosis just weeks after Fleetwood Mac bandmate Stevie Nicks sold her rights to Primary Wave for $100 million in late 2020. More than 160 songs are included in the publishing catalog he gave up, including a major songwriter credit on the eminently licensable “Go Your Own Way.”

6 Red Hot Chili Peppers ($145 million)

In May, the seminal alt-rockers sold their publishing catalog to Hipgnosis, but they aren’t giving it away just yet. According to insiders, the band received $140 million for a slew of tunes recorded over the course of a five-decade existence. The Red Hot Chili Peppers are rumored to be working on a new album that will be released later this year.

5 Ryan Tedder ($200 million)

The frontman of OneRepublic has also written singles for Adele, Taylor Swift, and U2. His $60 million catalog sale in 2017 was only a warm-up; in January, private-equity behemoth KKR purchased the vast majority of his remaining publishing copyrights, netting him just shy of $200 million. Tedder continues to make money in other ways: in August, OneRepublic released Human, its fifth studio album.

4 Kanye West ($250 million)

Despite the fact that the multihyphenate artist recently changed his legal name to “Ye,” his Yeezy footwear company accounted for the great majority of his earnings in the previous year. West’s new album Donda, which was released in August and is titled after his late mother, became his ninth consecutive number one solo album. And there’s more to come: West’s new Gap apparel deal could keep him near the top of this list for many years to come.

3 Paul Simon ($260 million)

In March of last year, the singer-songwriter sold his entire repertoire to Sony Music Publishing. The deal covered Simon’s solo work as well as the rights to songs he wrote with Simon and Garfunkel, totaling 400 works. His publishing rights were also merged with his master recordings, which were already at Sony.

2 Jay-Z ($470 million)

Although he came in second on this year’s list, hip-first hop’s billionaire just achieved his best annual earnings total in his career. He sold half of his Armand de Brignac champagne to luxury conglomerate LVMH for $300 million in February. A few months later, he and his Tidal co-owners sold 80 percent of the streaming service to Square for $302 million, which was originally purchased for $56 million. Partnerships with Puma and Tiffany & Co. are also part of his resume.

1 Bruce Springsteen ($590 million)

Bruce Springsteen sells publishing catalogs in a massive $500 million deal. In what may be the largest deal ever inked for a single musician’s body of work, Bruce Springsteen has reportedly sold his publishing catalog to Sony for $500 million. According to Billboard, The Boss has sold his masters to Sony Music — the parent company of Columbia Records, where Springsteen has released his music since his debut “Greetings From Asbury Park, N.J.” in 1973 — and his publishing to Sony Music Publishing.

We’ve looked at the highest-paid musicians of 2021. These are artists who have dominated streaming services, selling millions of albums and tickets to concerts around the world, as well as having a prolific music career spanning decades.
